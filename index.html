Amplitude
Hover to learn →
Maximum displacement from rest position. Larger amplitude = more energy. Tsunamis have tiny amplitude in deep water but enormous amplitude near shore.
Wavelength (λ)
Hover to learn →
Distance between two consecutive crests or troughs. Symbol λ (lambda). Tsunamis have wavelengths of 100–500 km — enormous compared to wind waves.
Frequency (f)
Hover to learn →
Cycles per second, measured in Hertz (Hz). f = 1/T. Tsunamis are very low frequency — periods of 10 minutes to 2 hours between wave arrivals.
Time Period (T)
Hover to learn →
Time for one complete wave cycle. T = 1/f. The longer the period, the more slowly the wave oscillates. Key to distinguishing tsunamis from wind waves.
Transverse Waves
Hover to learn →
Particles oscillate perpendicular to wave direction. Examples: light waves, electromagnetic waves, water surface waves (seismic S-waves).
Longitudinal Waves
Hover to learn →
Particles oscillate parallel to wave direction. Examples: sound waves, seismic P-waves (Primary waves). These travel faster than S-waves through rock.
Energy Transfer
Hover to learn →
Waves transfer energy without permanently moving matter. The wave pattern moves, not the water itself. A tsunami transfers earthquake energy across entire ocean basins.
Wave Speed
Hover to learn →
In shallow water, tsunami speed v = √(g·d). Deeper ocean → faster wave. Speed decreases near shore, causing wave height to increase dramatically (shoaling).

⚠️
PUBLIC TSUNAMI SAFETY GUIDE
If you receive a tsunami warning, you have minutes to act. Every second counts. This guide uses the same science behind WaveGuard's hybrid detection system.
📻
Step 1
Hear the Alert
Listen for official warnings via radio, TV, sirens, or mobile alerts. Do not wait to see the wave — it may be too late by then.
🏃
Step 2
Move Immediately
Go to high ground or inland. Move at least 1 mile from the coast or to a height of 30+ metres above sea level.
🗺️
Step 3
Follow Evacuation Routes
Use designated evacuation routes. Avoid bridges and low-lying roads near water. Do not take shortcuts through unfamiliar areas.
📱
Step 4
Stay Informed
Monitor official emergency channels. A tsunami can have multiple waves — the first may not be the largest. Stay updated.
⏳
Step 5
Wait for All-Clear
Do NOT return until authorities issue the official all-clear. The danger can last several hours after the first wave.
🤝
Step 6
Help Others
Help neighbours, especially elderly or disabled people. Alert others only if you can do so safely without delaying your own evacuation.
