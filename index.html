Teacher Questions — Main
Q: Tsunami speed formula?
v = √(g × d). At 4000m: √(9.81×4000) = 198 m/s = 713 km/h. At 10,000m: √(9.81×10000) = 313 m/s = 1,127 km/h.
Q: Why do seismic waves travel faster?
Rock is far more rigid and elastic than water. P-waves are longitudinal compression waves through Earth's crust at 5–14 km/s. Water cannot support these.
Q: P vs S waves?
P-waves: Primary, compressional, longitudinal, 14,000 km/h, through any medium. S-waves: Secondary, shear, transverse, 8,000 km/h, solid only.
Q: How does a DART buoy work?
Bottom Pressure Recorder on seafloor measures water pressure anomalies. Passes via acoustic modem to surface buoy → satellite → warning centre.
Q: Explain v = fλ?
Wave speed = frequency × wavelength. f = 0.0005 Hz, λ = 200,000m → v = 100 m/s. The equation is universal for all waves.
Advanced Questions
Q: Coastal speed change?
As depth decreases: v drops (v=√gd). But energy is conserved, so amplitude grows. Shoaling: 0.5m deep ocean → 15m at coast. Wavelength also compresses — waves stack up.
Q: How does hybrid reduce false alarms?
Only ~1 in 10 M6+ earthquakes generates a destructive tsunami. Seismic alone would alert constantly. Buoy confirmation acts as two-factor check — 85% fewer false alarms.
Q: Is Wavara real?
Wavara is a fictional Pacific island nation representing real coastal communities at risk. All physics, speeds, and methods are based on NOAA DART II and Pacific Tsunami Warning Centre.
Q: Future improvements?
AI pattern recognition, seafloor GPS (coseismic deformation), denser buoy networks, real-time ocean models for coastal run-up prediction.

Public Tsunami Safety Guide

Step 1
Hear the Alert
Listen for official warnings via radio, TV, sirens, or mobile alerts. Do not wait to see the wave.
🏃
Step 2
Move Immediately
High ground or inland. 1+ miles from coast or 30 metres above sea level. You have 6.5 hours.
🚗
Step 3
Evacuation Routes
Follow designated routes. Avoid bridges and low-lying roads near water.
📱
Step 4
Stay Informed
Monitor official channels. Multiple waves may follow — the first may not be largest.
⏳
Step 5
Wait for All-Clear
Do NOT return until official all-clear. Threat can last hours after first wave.
🤝
Step 6
Help Others
Help elderly and disabled neighbours. You have 6.5 hours — use it to help your community.
