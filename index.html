Why Seismic Arrives First

Seismic P-waves travel through solid rock at 3,000–8,000 m/s (≈14,000 km/h). The tsunami travels as a shallow-water wave at ~1,000 km/h. That is a 14× speed difference. This is why hybrid systems work: the faster signal gives early warning, the slower signal gives confirmation.

Why Hybrid Reduces False Alarms

Only ~1 in 10 M6+ earthquakes generates a destructive tsunami. Seismic alone causes constant false alarms (see 1946 Hilo — destroyed public trust). Buoy confirmation acts as a two-factor check. Hybrid = 85% fewer false alarms than seismic-only.

Wave Physics
Amplitude
Deep-ocean tsunami amplitude is just 0.3–1m — ships cannot detect it. Near shore, energy conservation (shoaling) amplifies it to 10–40m. Energy ∝ Amplitude². This is why tsunamis are catastrophic at the coast but invisible in the open ocean.
Wavelength (λ)
Tsunami λ = 200–500 km. Wind wave λ ≈ 100m. Because λ is so much larger than ocean depth, the entire water column moves when the seabed shifts — catastrophic energy. This makes tsunamis shallow-water waves regardless of ocean depth.
v = fλ and v = √(gd)
At depth 4,000m: v = √(9.81×4000) = 198 m/s = 713 km/h. At 10,000m: v = √(9.81×10000) = 313 m/s = 1,127 km/h. Deeper ocean = faster tsunami. The wave slows near shore and rises dramatically.
P-waves vs S-waves
P-waves are longitudinal compression waves — travel fastest through rigid rock at 14,000 km/h. S-waves are transverse shear waves, slower, cannot travel through liquids. Our sensors detect P-waves for speed. S-waves confirm the quake type.
Energy Transfer
Waves transfer energy, not matter. Buoys bob in place as a 1,000 km/h tsunami passes beneath — the energy travels 7,000 km, the water particles barely move horizontally. This is observable: corks float up/down but don't travel with the wave.

Core Questions
Q: What is the tsunami speed formula?
v = √(g × d). At 4,000m: √(9.81×4000) = 198 m/s = 713 km/h. At 10,000m: √(9.81×10000) = 313 m/s ≈ 1,127 km/h. Depth determines speed — deeper = faster.
Q: Why do seismic waves travel faster than the tsunami?
Rock is far more rigid and elastic than water. P-waves are longitudinal compression waves through Earth's crust at 5–14 km/s. Water cannot support the same elastic wave propagation — tsunamis are limited by gravity and depth.
Q: What is the difference between P-waves and S-waves?
P-waves: Primary, compressional, longitudinal, ~14,000 km/h, travel through any medium. S-waves: Secondary, shear, transverse, ~8,000 km/h, solids only. P-waves arrive first — hence "Primary." S-waves cannot travel through the ocean.
Q: How does a DART buoy work?
A Bottom Pressure Recorder (BPR) sits on the seafloor and measures water pressure anomalies. Data passes via acoustic modem to a surface buoy, then by satellite to the warning centre. Response time: ~15 minutes from detection to alert.
Q: Explain v = fλ.
Wave speed = frequency × wavelength. For a tsunami: f ≈ 0.0005 Hz (one wave every 33 minutes), λ ≈ 200,000m, so v = 0.0005 × 200,000 = 100 m/s = 360 km/h. The equation is universal for all wave types.
Advanced Questions
Q: What happens to tsunami speed near the coast?
As depth (d) decreases, v = √(gd) decreases. But energy is conserved, so amplitude increases — wave shoaling. A 0.5m deep-ocean wave becomes 15m at shore. Wavelength compresses, waves stack up. This is why tsunamis appear to "grow" as they approach land.
Q: How does the hybrid system reduce false alarms?
Only ~1 in 10 M6+ earthquakes generates a destructive tsunami. Seismic-only would alert after every large quake. Buoy confirmation acts as two-factor verification — the tsunami must physically form and begin propagating before the public alert fires. This reduces false alarms by approximately 85%.
Q: Is Wavara a real place?
Wavara is a fictional Pacific island nation created for this project. It represents real at-risk coastal communities like Samoa, Tonga, and Pacific Island nations. All physics, speeds, distances, and methods are based on real NOAA DART II systems and Pacific Tsunami Warning Centre methodology.
Q: What are future improvements to hybrid systems?
AI pattern recognition for faster seismic classification, seafloor GPS networks (coseismic deformation mapping), denser buoy networks, real-time ocean model integration for coastal run-up prediction, and machine-learning false-alarm filtering.
